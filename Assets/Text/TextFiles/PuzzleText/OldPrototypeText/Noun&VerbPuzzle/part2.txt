For every success story there are thousands of indie films that go unwatched . The digital age has made it easier than ever to break through the clutter of entertainment options to an audience . Chris Moore, a producer of " Manchester by the Sea" compares the 
 output of indie films now to trees falling in the forest . " nobody is making dollar off this business " , he says . Mr Moore may be dramatising but only a little . Indie films have always been a risky bet for investors . Since 2002 the median return on investments at the box 
 office for films released in north america with budgets of less than 10 million dollars has been 45 cents to the dollar .
